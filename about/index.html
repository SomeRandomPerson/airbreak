Trammell Hudson

I'm a hardware and firmware security researcher who likes to take things apart. Ten years ago I started the Magic Lantern firmware project, which allowed any one to write their own extensions for their Canon digital SLR cameras. Five years ago I discovered the Thunderstrike vulnerability in MacBook firmware and have continued to research other early-boot time security issues. More recently I've been developing the open source corebot based Heads firmware for slightly more secure laptops as well as the LinuxBoot open source firmware for servers.

More of my projects are on trmm.net. You can send me encrypted email with the GPG public key a4a643fc5a5b326a541a3be111dba768aaaedafb or contact me via @qrs.
